Acid-Test Ratio - us

Ratio: ([ca] less [inv]) divided by [cl] This is a variant of the [crat]; it only includes items which are quickly converted into current assets. It is called ‘Acid-Test’ because it measures the ability to meet unexpected demands without depending on the sale of inventory. Also called Quick Ratio. A Quick Ratio of 1 is a good benchmark. Higher ratios indicate a satisfactory condition. Decreasing ratios indicate either a deteriorating cash position or a deteriorating demand for products. Ratios below ½ indicate a potentially severe situation like slow moving inventory and a higher chance of bankruptcy.
